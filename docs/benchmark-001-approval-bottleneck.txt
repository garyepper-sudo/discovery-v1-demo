Company: Acme Manufacturing

Over the last six months, nearly every customer implementation has finished later than originally promised.

Project managers say work frequently sits idle while waiting for executive approval.

Department managers cannot approve contracts larger than $5,000 without CEO review.

Engineering managers report that feature requests often wait several days for prioritization decisions.

Sales representatives say customers become frustrated because implementation dates continually move.

Several employees mentioned that meetings often end with "we'll wait until the CEO decides."

Operations managers estimate that approximately 25% of project time is spent waiting for approvals.

Customer Success has documented an increase in complaints related to delayed onboarding.

Employees generally agree that leadership wants high quality decisions, but many believe decision-making has become overly centralized.

No evidence suggests employees are unwilling to work.
Most delays appear to occur while waiting for decisions rather than performing work.
