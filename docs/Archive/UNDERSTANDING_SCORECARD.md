# Discovery Organizational Understanding Scorecard

## Purpose

Discovery exists to help organizations become progressively more self-aware.

Every sprint should improve Discovery's ability to transform organizational experience into enduring organizational understanding.

This scorecard is the objective measure of whether we are moving toward that goal.

It is intentionally independent of implementation details.

We do not measure engines.

We measure organizational understanding.

---

# North Star

> Discovery continuously transforms organizational experience into enduring organizational understanding.

Every architectural decision should improve one or more dimensions below.

---

# Sprint Evaluation

At the end of every sprint answer four questions.

## What changed?

Example

Persistent observations now survive across investigations.

---

## Which dimensions improved?

Example

Continuity

6 → 7

Integration

4 → 5

---

## Did anything become worse?

Example

Explainability decreased because new memory objects are difficult to trace.

---

## Was this sprint worth it?

Only answer YES if Discovery became measurably better at organizational understanding.

Otherwise the sprint should be reconsidered.

---

# Design Rule

We do not build engines for the sake of building engines.

Every engine must measurably improve at least one dimension of organizational understanding.

If an engine cannot improve this scorecard, it does not belong in Discovery.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Discovery is not attempting to become an AI assistant.

Discovery is attempting to become an increasingly accurate model of an organization's evolving understanding of itself.

Every sprint should move Discovery closer to that objective.

Every line of code should help an organization become slightly more self-aware than it was yesterday.
