# Living Organization Benchmark

**Purpose**

This document defines Discovery's ultimate development objective.

Discovery is not intended to become merely an organizational analytics system.

Discovery is intended to become a **Living Organizational Model**.

This benchmark measures Discovery's progress toward that objective.

Unlike implementation benchmarks, this benchmark measures **organizational cognition**.

Every sprint should improve one or more dimensions of this benchmark.

---

# Core Principle

A living organizational model should continuously:

- perceive,
- understand,
- remember,
- learn,
- explain,
- predict,
- adapt,
- simulate,
- and improve itself.

The benchmark measures Discovery's progress toward that capability.

---

# Level 1 — Perception

## Question

Can Discovery accurately perceive organizational experience?

### Measures

- Evidence ingestion
- Observation quality
- Signal detection
- Semantic understanding
- Entity recognition
- Contradiction detection

### Success

Discovery consistently distinguishes meaningful organizational signals from noise.

# Level 10 — Organizational Self-Awareness

## Question

Does Discovery understand its own understanding?

### Measures

Discovery should continuously answer:

- What do I know?
- What don't I know?
- What am I uncertain about?
- What would change my mind?
- Where should I learn next?
- What are my blind spots?
- How has my understanding evolved?
- How healthy is my understanding?

### Success

Discovery continuously evaluates the quality and limitations of its own organizational understanding.

# North Star

Discovery should continuously evolve toward becoming a true living organizational model.

Every sprint should answer one question:

> **Did this work make Discovery more like a living organization?**

If the answer is no, the work should be reconsidered.

---

# Relationship to Other Benchmarks

This benchmark is Discovery's highest-level benchmark.

Supporting benchmarks include:

- Atlas
- Benchmark Integrity
- Understanding Scorecard
- Executive Projection Coverage
- Cognitive Fitness Profile

These benchmarks measure implementation quality.

The Living Organization Benchmark measures Discovery's long-term evolution.

---

# Canonical Development Principle

> Discovery should not be evaluated by the number of features it contains.

> Discovery should be evaluated by how closely it resembles a living organizational model.

Every architectural decision, reasoning engine, executive capability, and user experience should move Discovery closer to that objective.
