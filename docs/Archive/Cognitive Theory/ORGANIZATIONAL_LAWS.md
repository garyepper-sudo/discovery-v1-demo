# Discovery Organizational Laws

**Status:** Canonical Proposal

---

# Purpose

This document defines Discovery's canonical understanding of how organizations behave.

These laws become the executable knowledge base that powers:

- Organizational Understanding
- Prediction
- Executive Planning
- Organizational Simulation
- Executive Recommendations
- Organizational Learning

Unlike software rules, Organizational Laws are **hypotheses**.

Discovery continuously validates, strengthens, weakens, and refines these laws through evidence and organizational outcomes.

---

# Philosophy

Organizations are adaptive systems.

No organizational problem exists in isolation.

Every meaningful organizational change influences multiple connected systems.

Discovery therefore reasons through **organizational laws** rather than isolated observations.

---

# Organizational Law

An Organizational Law is a reusable explanation describing how one organizational system influences another.

Every law answers:

> If this changes...
>
> what else changes,
>
> why,
>
> and with what confidence?

---

# Canonical Structure

Every Organizational Law contains:

- Source System
- Target System
- Influence Direction
- Relative Influence Strength
- Confidence
- Executive Explanation
- Supporting Mechanisms
- Supporting Beliefs
- Supporting Theories
- Supporting Evidence
- Validation History

Organizational Laws are executable.

Prediction, Planning, and Simulation all consume the same laws.

# Foundational Organizational Laws

The following laws become Discovery's initial organizational physics.

These laws are expected to evolve.

---

# LAW-001

Decision Flow Improves Coordination

```
Decision Flow

↓

Coordination
```

Principle

Organizations coordinate more effectively when decisions are made quickly, clearly, and consistently.

Reason

Decision latency increases waiting, uncertainty, duplicated work, and coordination overhead.

Expected Organizational Effects

Improved Decision Flow should:

- reduce ambiguity
- reduce coordination delay
- improve execution throughput

# Law Confidence

Confidence measures Discovery's belief that the relationship exists.

Confidence does not measure causal strength.

Discovery should continuously update confidence through:

- evidence
- prediction evaluation
- intervention outcomes
- simulation validation

# Intervention

Executive interventions never modify the organizational model directly.

Instead:

```
Executive Decision

↓

Intervention

↓

Affected Organizational Laws

↓

Propagation

↓

Future Organizational State
```

Discovery therefore reasons from organizational laws rather than software logic.

---

# Prediction

```
↓

Organizational Laws

```

---

# Simulation

Simulation applies interventions to Organizational Laws.

```
Current Organization

↓

Intervention

↓

Organizational Laws

↓

Propagation

↓

Future Organization
```

Simulation therefore becomes organizational physics in motion.

---

# Planning

Planning traverses Organizational Laws backwards.

Example

```
Goal

Improve Execution

↓

Execution Capacity

↓

Coordination

↓

Decision Flow

↓

Possible Interventions
```

Planning becomes inverse simulation.

---

# Recommendation

Recommendations identify interventions that produce the highest expected organizational leverage.

Discovery should continuously ask:

- Which organizational law has the greatest downstream influence?
- Which intervention changes that law?
- Which intervention satisfies executive constraints?
- Which intervention has the highest expected organizational benefit?

---

# Learning

Discovery continuously improves Organizational Laws.

When predictions are correct:

- strengthen confidence

When predictions are incorrect:

- weaken confidence

When new evidence appears:

- revise relationships

When repeated observations emerge:

- create new laws

# Canonical Principle

Discovery should never hardcode organizational behavior.

Discovery should continuously learn organizational behavior.

Organizational Laws become Discovery's reusable, explainable, and continuously improving understanding of how organizations work.

They are the foundation upon which Prediction, Planning, Simulation, Recommendation, and Decision Intelligence are built.
