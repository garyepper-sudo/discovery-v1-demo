# Discovery Product State

**Status:** Canonical Product Snapshot

This document is the canonical snapshot of Discovery's current product state.

Every product sprint should begin here.

Read this document before making product, UX, architecture, or implementation decisions.

The **Product Canon** defines what Discovery is.

This document defines where Discovery currently is.

---

# Discovery

**Status:** Executive Operating System MVP

---

# Product Identity

Discovery is an **Executive Operating System**.

Its purpose is to continuously improve executive judgment.

Discovery does not replace executive decision-making.

It improves executive decision-making through a continuously evolving **Operating Model** that represents how an organization actually works.

Discovery is not:

- a dashboard,
- a chatbot,
- a reporting platform,
- a document repository,
- a project management system,
- a business intelligence platform,
- or an autonomous executive.

Everything Discovery produces should strengthen executive judgment.

---

# Current Product Vision

Discovery should become the place executive teams naturally go to understand their organization, evaluate important decisions, and continuously improve organizational performance.

The product should feel like an experienced executive advisor that:

- understands the organization,
- explains its reasoning,
- evaluates alternatives,
- simulates consequences,
- learns from outcomes,
- continuously improves future executive judgment.

The Operating Model is the foundation of that experience.

---

# The Operating Model

The Operating Model is Discovery's most valuable product asset.

It represents Discovery's evolving understanding of:

- organizational reality,
- organizational conditions,
- executive constraints,
- strategic objectives,
- organizational dependencies,
- executive decisions,
- initiatives,
- historical outcomes,
- executive learning,
- persistent organizational memory.

Everything Discovery produces originates from the Operating Model.

Including:

- executive understanding,
- recommendations,
- simulations,
- confidence,
- executive communication,
- decision support,
- organizational learning.

Every product improvement should either:

- strengthen the Operating Model,
- or expose it more effectively.

Executives should always understand that improving the Operating Model improves every future recommendation.

# Current Product Workflow

Discovery now supports one complete executive workflow.

```text
Executive Brief

↓

Decision Definition
(Executive Intent Confirmation)

↓

Decision Lab

↓

Commit Decision

↓

Decision Tracking

↓

Executive Review

↓

Executive Learning

↓

Operating Model Improvement
```

This workflow is now considered the canonical executive experience.

Future work should refine this experience rather than introduce additional workflows.

---

# Executive Brief

The Executive Brief answers:

> What should I pay attention to?

Discovery should immediately communicate:

- what it believes,
- why it matters,
- what deserves executive attention,
- what action it recommends.

Executives should understand the situation in seconds.

---

# Decision Definition

Decision Definition exists to confirm executive intent.

It is **not** a traditional form.

Discovery already understands:

- organizational context,
- evidence,
- recommendations,
- organizational constraints,
- executive reasoning.

Executives primarily confirm or adjust intent before entering Decision Lab.

The experience should require minimal effort.

---

# Decision Lab

Decision Lab is Discovery's primary executive reasoning environment.

It should help executives:

- compare strategic alternatives,
- challenge assumptions,
- explore simulations,
- understand trade-offs,
- evaluate organizational impact,
- commit decisions confidently.

Decision Lab consumes existing cognition.

It never recreates reasoning already performed by the Executive Cognitive Operating System.

---

# Executive Conversation

Discovery should follow the natural sequence of executive thinking.

```text
What should I do?

↓

Why?

↓

What else could I do?

↓

What could make this wrong?

↓

What happens if...?

↓

Are we ready?

↓

How is execution progressing?

↓

What did we learn?
```

The product should naturally guide executives through this conversation.

---

# Current Product Focus

Discovery has completed its first-generation Executive Cognitive Operating System.

The architecture is considered stable.

Current development is no longer focused on expanding reasoning capabilities.

Current priorities are:

1. Improve executive usability.
2. Reduce executive reading.
3. Improve recommendation quality.
4. Improve executive communication.
5. Improve executive confidence.
6. Improve simulation usability.
7. Improve decision workflow.
8. Strengthen Executive Learning.
9. Increase Operating Model visibility.
10. Validate executive behavior with real customers.

The current bottleneck is executive product quality—not reasoning capability.

---

# UX Philosophy

Discovery should always minimize executive effort.

Executives should spend their time making decisions—not entering information.

Discovery should infer organizational context whenever possible.

Complexity should remain hidden until requested.

Progressive disclosure should be the default interaction model.

---

# Progressive Disclosure

Information should unfold naturally.

### Level 1

Recommendation

### Level 2

Why

### Level 3

Alternatives

### Level 4

Challenge

### Level 5

Supporting Evidence

Executives should never feel overwhelmed by Discovery's reasoning.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature should improve one or more of:

- executive judgment,
- executive confidence,
- executive understanding,
- recommendation quality,
- Operating Model quality,
- organizational learning,
- executive trust.

Features that do not strengthen these outcomes should not be built.

---

# Current Validation Goal

The objective is not feature expansion.

The objective is validating that Discovery genuinely improves executive judgment.

Questions to answer include:

- Does Discovery reduce executive effort?
- Do executives trust Discovery?
- Do executives naturally begin important work inside Discovery?
- Do executives challenge recommendations?
- Do executives explore simulations?
- Do executives commit decisions with greater confidence?
- Do executives return for reviews?
- Does Executive Learning improve future recommendations?
- Does the Operating Model become stronger over time?

Executive behavior—not feature count—drives product evolution.

---

# Current Onboarding Philosophy

Discovery should create value before requesting organizational information.

The onboarding sequence should be:

```text
Create Immediate Value

↓

Earn Trust

↓

Improve One Important Decision

↓

Strengthen the Operating Model

↓

Request Additional Organizational Information
```

Discovery earns organizational understanding.

It never demands it.

---

# Confidence Philosophy

Confidence exists to improve executive judgment.

Discovery should always explain:

- why confidence is high,
- what limits confidence,
- which assumptions remain,
- what evidence could improve confidence,
- how confidence changes across alternatives.

Confidence should increase understanding—not persuasion.

---

# Observable Cognition

Discovery should expose genuine cognition occurring inside the Executive Cognitive Operating System.

Executives should be able to observe:

- organizational understanding,
- recommendation synthesis,
- constraint identification,
- simulation comparison,
- confidence calibration,
- organizational learning,
- Operating Model improvement.

Observable cognition should strengthen executive trust while minimizing reading.

---

# Long-Term Product Vision

Every completed decision should improve the Operating Model.

Every Executive Review should improve Executive Learning.

Every Executive Learning cycle should improve Persistent Organizational Memory.

Persistent Organizational Memory should improve Longitudinal Executive Judgment.

A stronger Operating Model produces:

- better understanding,
- better recommendations,
- better simulations,
- better confidence,
- better executive decisions,
- better organizational outcomes.

Discovery should become more valuable every time an organization uses it.

---

# Product Success

Discovery succeeds when executive teams naturally begin important work inside Discovery.

Discovery succeeds when executives trust its recommendations.

Discovery succeeds when organizations continuously improve their Operating Model.

Discovery succeeds when every completed decision improves future executive judgment.

Everything else supports that objective.
