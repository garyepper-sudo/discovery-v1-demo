# Discovery Decision Object

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines the canonical product object inside Discovery.

Discovery does not organize itself around pages.

Discovery organizes itself around Decisions.

Every executive workflow ultimately contributes to creating, improving, recording, reviewing, or learning from a Decision.

If another product object conflicts with this one, the Decision object is correct.

---

# Product Philosophy

Organizations do not purchase Discovery to create reports.

Organizations purchase Discovery to improve decisions.

Therefore every significant capability inside Discovery should ultimately support one Decision.

---

# Canonical Lifecycle

Every Decision progresses through the same lifecycle.

```
Question

Understanding

↓

↓

↓

↓

↓

↓

Implementation

↓

↓

↓

```

This lifecycle defines the executive experience.

---

# Stage 1

## Question

Everything begins with a business question.

Examples:

- Should we hire?
- Should we expand?
- Should we reorganize?
- Should we acquire?
- Why is execution slowing?

Discovery begins with executive intent rather than documents.

---

# Stage 2

## Current Understanding

Discovery forms its current understanding.

This answers:

> What is happening?

The Understanding contains:

- executive narrative,
- supporting reasoning,
- confidence,
- major assumptions,
- business impact.

---

# Stage 3

## Recommendation

Discovery proposes one or more possible courses of action.

Recommendations are not commands.

Recommendations represent Discovery's current reasoning.

Every recommendation must explain itself.

---

# Stage 4

## Challenge

Before moving toward a decision, Discovery should strengthen executive thinking.

Discovery should:

- expose assumptions,
- present competing explanations,
- identify second-order effects,
- highlight opportunity cost,
- perform stress testing.

Discovery should improve reasoning before improving confidence.

---

# Stage 5

## Simulation

Simulation explores possible futures.

Simulation exists to answer:

> What happens if we do this?

Simulation should improve executive understanding rather than simply generate forecasts.

---

# Stage 6

## Decision Confidence

Confidence measures Discovery's confidence in the current recommendation.

Confidence should always answer:

- Why not higher?
- Which assumption matters most?
- What information would improve confidence?
- How much improvement is expected?

Confidence should always remain attached to a specific conclusion.

---

# Stage 7

## Executive Decision

Executives own decisions.

Discovery never makes decisions.

Discovery supports them.

The Decision captures:

- final decision,
- executive rationale,
- assumptions,
- expected outcomes,
- confidence at commitment.

---

# Stage 8

## Implementation

Discovery records implementation.

Implementation begins organizational learning.

Discovery should continue monitoring signals after commitment.

---

# Stage 9

## Review

At an appropriate future date Discovery reviews the decision.

Review compares:

Expected outcome

↓

Actual outcome

↓

Variance

↓

Explanation

Discovery should ask thoughtful questions rather than simply collect results.

---

# Stage 10

## Reflection

Executives contribute learning.

Reflection should capture:

- surprises,
- mistakes,
- successful assumptions,
- failed assumptions,
- future advice.

Reflection improves organizational judgment.

---

# Stage 11

## Learning

Every completed Decision improves Discovery.

Discovery should continuously improve:

- organizational understanding,
- simulations,
- confidence,
- recommendations,
- executive guidance.

Learning is the final stage of every Decision.

# Product Goal

Discovery succeeds when organizations naturally begin every important decision inside Discovery.

The Decision is therefore the canonical product object around which every future capability should evolve.
