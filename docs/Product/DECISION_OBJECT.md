# Discovery Executive Work Object

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines the canonical product object inside Discovery.

Discovery does not organize itself around pages.

Discovery organizes itself around **Executive Work**.

Executive Work represents every important strategic activity an executive team is actively considering, executing, tracking, or reviewing.

Executive Work includes:

- executive decisions,
- strategic initiatives,
- Discovery-generated recommendations,
- executive-created work.

Every executive workflow ultimately contributes to creating, improving, committing, tracking, reviewing, or learning from Executive Work.

If another product object conflicts with this one, the Executive Work object is correct.

---

# Product Philosophy

Organizations do not purchase Discovery to create reports.

Organizations purchase Discovery to improve how important work is executed.

Discovery should become the operating system organizations naturally use for Executive Work.

Every significant capability inside Discovery should ultimately strengthen one Executive Work item.

---

# Executive Work Origins

Executive Work originates from two sources.

## Discovery-generated

Discovery continuously identifies opportunities including:

- recommendations,
- organizational risks,
- strategic opportunities,
- suggested initiatives,
- suggested executive decisions.

## Executive-generated

Executives may create work independently of Discovery.

Examples include:

- acquisitions,
- reorganizations,
- product launches,
- pricing decisions,
- hiring strategies,
- budgeting,
- operational improvements,
- strategic initiatives.

Both sources become identical once they enter Discovery.

This lifecycle defines Discovery's executive experience.

---

# Stage 1

## Executive Work

Everything begins with meaningful executive work.

Examples:

- Should we hire?
- Should we expand?
- Should we reorganize?
- Should we acquire?
- Launch European expansion.
- Improve onboarding.
- Reduce customer churn.

Discovery begins with executive intent rather than documents.

---

# Stage 2

## Understanding

Discovery forms its current organizational understanding.

This answers:

> What is happening?

Understanding includes:

- executive narrative,
- organizational conditions,
- primary executive judgment,
- supporting reasoning,
- confidence,
- assumptions,
- business impact.

The Operating Model forms this understanding.

---

# Stage 3

## Recommendation

Discovery proposes one or more possible courses of action.

Recommendations are never commands.

Recommendations represent Discovery's current reasoning.

Every recommendation must explain itself.

Executives should immediately understand:

- what Discovery recommends,
- why,
- expected organizational impact.

---

# Stage 4

## Challenge

Discovery strengthens executive thinking before commitment.

Discovery should expose:

- assumptions,
- competing explanations,
- competing strategies,
- uncertainty,
- confidence limiters,
- evidence that could change the recommendation,
- important risks.

Challenge improves judgment.

Not confidence.

---

# Stage 5

## Simulation

Simulation explores alternative futures.

Simulation answers:

> What happens if we choose this path?

Simulation should:

- compare interventions,
- evaluate tradeoffs,
- propagate organizational effects,
- identify downstream consequences,
- improve executive understanding.

Simulation exists to improve decisions.

---

# Stage 6

## Commitment

Executives own commitments.

Discovery never makes decisions.

Discovery supports them.

Commitment records:

- selected strategy,
- executive rationale,
- assumptions,
- expected outcomes,
- commitment confidence,
- commitment date.

This marks the transition from reasoning to execution.

---

# Stage 7

## Tracking

Discovery continuously monitors Executive Work.

Tracking compares:

Expected

↓

Current Reality

Discovery should monitor:

- initiative progress,
- organizational conditions,
- confidence,
- prediction performance,
- emerging risks,
- execution quality.

Tracking transforms Executive Work into a living process rather than a static record.

---

# Stage 8

## Review

At an appropriate future date Discovery performs a Decision Review.

Review compares:

```text
Expected Outcome

↓

Observed Outcome

↓

Variance

↓

Explanation
```

Discovery should explain:

- what happened,
- what surprised the organization,
- what assumptions held,
- what assumptions failed.

---

# Stage 9

## Learning

Every completed Executive Work item strengthens the Operating Model.

Learning improves:

- organizational understanding,
- beliefs,
- theories,
- prediction calibration,
- confidence calibration,
- simulations,
- future recommendations.

Learning is the final stage of every Executive Work lifecycle.

---

# Operating Model

The Operating Model is the brain behind every Executive Work item.

Executive Work does not create reasoning.

The Operating Model does.

Every recommendation, simulation, confidence estimate, and learning event should ultimately be explainable through the Operating Model.

The relationship is:

```text
Operating Model

↓

Executive Understanding

↓

Executive Work

↓

Execution

↓

Reality

↓

Learning

↓

Operating Model Improves
```

This flywheel is Discovery's primary competitive advantage.

# Product Goal

Discovery succeeds when organizations naturally begin, manage, review, and improve every important piece of Executive Work inside Discovery.

The Operating Model continuously improves through this process.

Executive Work is therefore the canonical product object around which every future capability should evolve.
