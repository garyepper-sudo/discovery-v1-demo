# Discovery Product Philosophy

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines the long-term product philosophy of Discovery.

It answers one question:

> **What is Discovery becoming?**

Unlike sprint documents, this document changes rarely.

It establishes the principles that should remain stable as Discovery evolves.

Every product decision should be evaluated against this philosophy.

---

# Product Identity

Discovery is an **Executive Operating System**.

Its purpose is to continuously improve the quality of executive judgment.

Discovery does not replace executive decision-making.

Discovery improves executive decision-making by building and continuously evolving an accurate understanding of how an organization actually works.

---

# The Operating Model

The Operating Model is the foundation of Discovery.

It is the organization's continuously evolving cognitive model.

Everything Discovery produces originates from the Operating Model.

The Operating Model is the "brain" behind:

- organizational understanding,
- recommendations,
- simulations,
- executive decisions,
- initiatives,
- confidence,
- learning.

As Discovery learns, the Operating Model becomes progressively more accurate.

Every recommendation should ultimately be explainable through the Operating Model.

---

# Executive Work

Discovery manages Executive Work.

Executive Work represents the strategic activities that executives are actively considering, executing, or evaluating.

Executive Work originates from two sources.

## Discovery-generated

Discovery continuously identifies opportunities including:

- recommendations,
- organizational risks,
- emerging constraints,
- optimization opportunities,
- suggested initiatives,
- suggested executive decisions.

## Executive-generated

Executives may create work independently of Discovery.

Examples include:

- acquisitions,
- reorganizations,
- hiring plans,
- product launches,
- budgeting,
- pricing,
- strategic initiatives,
- operational changes.

Discovery reasons about both equally.

# Discovery Conversation

Discovery should feel like a conversation with a trusted executive advisor.

Executives naturally ask questions in a predictable order.

Discovery should answer them in that same order.

## 1. What should I do?

Provide one clear recommendation.

## 2. Why?

Explain the recommendation.

## 3. What else could I do?

Compare alternative strategies.

## 4. What could make this wrong?

Surface assumptions, uncertainty, risks, and evidence that could change the recommendation.

## 5. What happens if...?

Simulate alternative futures.

## 6. What happened?

Track execution and compare expected outcomes with observed outcomes.

## 7. How do we improve?

Learn from reality and continuously improve future decisions.

---

# Progressive Disclosure

Discovery minimizes reading.

Executives should never be overwhelmed with information.

Information should be revealed progressively.

Level 1

Executive summary.

Level 2

Reasoning.

Level 3

Alternatives.

Level 4

Evidence.

Level 5

Complete supporting analysis.

Complexity should always be optional.

---

# Executive Confidence

Discovery should continuously answer:

> Why should I trust this recommendation?

Confidence is not a single score.

Confidence is supported by:

- evidence,
- mechanisms,
- theories,
- simulations,
- historical prediction accuracy,
- prior decision performance.

Confidence should improve over time.

---

# Observable Cognition

Discovery should never pretend to think.

Instead, it should expose genuine cognition occurring inside the Operating Model.

Examples include:

- Building organizational understanding
- Evaluating mechanisms
- Updating theories
- Ranking interventions
- Propagating organizational effects
- Simulating futures
- Calibrating confidence

Executives should see real reasoning, not artificial animations.

---

# Operating Model Visibility

The Operating Model should always remain visible throughout the product.

Executives should understand that every recommendation originates from the Operating Model.

Uploading additional information should visibly improve the Operating Model.

Examples include:

- confidence increases,
- new concepts formed,
- stronger theories,
- improved predictions,
- improved simulations.

The product should reinforce the relationship:

```text
More organizational understanding

↓

Better Operating Model

↓

Better recommendations

↓

Better executive decisions
```

---

# Learning Flywheel

Every completed decision strengthens Discovery.

Reality becomes feedback.

Feedback improves:

- beliefs,
- theories,
- prediction calibration,
- confidence calibration,
- simulations,
- future recommendations.

The system becomes progressively more valuable through use.

---

# Product Design Principles

Every feature should improve one or more of:

- organizational understanding,
- executive confidence,
- decision quality,
- organizational learning,
- operating model quality.

Features that do not improve these outcomes should not be built.

---

# Product North Star

Discovery succeeds when executives regularly think:

> "Our Operating Model understands the organization better today than it did yesterday."

Because a better Operating Model produces:

- better recommendations,
- better simulations,
- better executive decisions,
- better organizational outcomes.

Everything in Discovery exists to continuously improve the Operating Model.
