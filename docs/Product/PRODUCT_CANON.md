# Discovery Product Canon

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines the product philosophy of Discovery.

Every product decision, UX decision, feature, workflow, and engineering investment should support this document.

This document answers one question:

> **What is Discovery?**

If another document contradicts this one, this document is correct.

---

# Mission

Discovery exists to help organizations continuously improve the quality of their decisions.

Discovery does not replace executive judgment.

Discovery improves it.

---

# Product Vision

Organizations make thousands of decisions.

Very few organizations systematically improve how they make those decisions.

Discovery creates an organizational decision system that becomes more valuable after every important decision.

Every completed decision should improve the next decision.

---

# Product Philosophy

Discovery is not:

- a dashboard
- a chatbot
- a document repository
- a reporting system
- a business intelligence platform

Discovery is an Executive Decision Workspace.

Its purpose is to help executive teams:

- understand complex situations,
- challenge assumptions,
- explore alternatives,
- improve confidence,
- make better decisions,
- and continuously learn from outcomes.

---

# The Core Workflow

Every significant interaction inside Discovery follows the same workflow.

```
Question

↓

Understanding

↓

Recommendation

↓

Challenge

↓

Simulation

↓

Improve Confidence

↓

Decision

↓

Review

↓

Learning
```

This workflow is the foundation of the product.

# The Decision Is The Product

Discovery does not organize itself around pages.

Discovery organizes itself around Decisions.

Every page is simply another perspective on the current decision.

The Decision is the canonical product object.

---

# Discovery's Role

Discovery is not responsible for making executive decisions.

Executives own decisions.

Discovery owns:

- organizational understanding,
- reasoning,
- simulation,
- confidence,
- learning.

Discovery should always feel like a trusted executive thought partner rather than an automated decision maker.

---

# Trust

Trust is Discovery's most valuable product feature.

Discovery earns trust by:

- explaining its reasoning,
- exposing uncertainty,
- recommending validation,
- admitting limitations,
- learning from outcomes.

Discovery should never imply certainty where uncertainty exists.

---

# Confidence

Confidence should always be attached to a specific conclusion.

Discovery should not communicate generic organizational confidence.

Instead Discovery communicates:

- confidence in the current understanding,
- confidence in the recommendation,
- remaining assumptions,
- the confidence gap,
- and the highest-value validation opportunity.

---

# Learning

Discovery begins learning immediately.

Learning does not begin after implementation.

Discovery continuously learns from:

- executive feedback,
- disagreement,
- simulation choices,
- evidence acquisition,
- committed decisions,
- implementation,
- outcome reviews,
- executive reflection.

Every interaction should improve future decisions.

---

# Organizational Memory

Discovery should become the organizational memory for important decisions.

Every significant decision should become a permanent organizational asset.

Discovery should remember:

- why the decision was made,
- what assumptions existed,
- what was predicted,
- what actually happened,
- and what was learned.

Organizations should become progressively better decision makers because Discovery remembers what organizations forget.

---

# Product Flywheel

Discovery should become more valuable every time it is used.

```
Better Questions

↓

Better Understanding

↓

Better Decisions

↓

More Decisions

↓

More Learning

↓

Better Organizational Model

↓

Higher Trust

↓

Better Questions
```

The product should improve through usage rather than through manual configuration.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature should satisfy at least one of the following.

- Improve understanding.
- Improve confidence.
- Improve decisions.
- Improve organizational learning.

Features that do not improve one of these outcomes should not be built.

---

# Product Success

Discovery succeeds when executive teams naturally begin important decisions inside Discovery.

Discovery succeeds when organizations become better decision makers over time.

Discovery succeeds when every important decision improves the next one.

That is the purpose of the platform.
