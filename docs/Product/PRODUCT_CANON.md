# Discovery Product Canon

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines Discovery's permanent product philosophy.

It answers one question:

> **What is Discovery?**

Every product decision, engineering investment, workflow, executive experience, benchmark, and customer validation effort should reinforce the principles defined here.

Unlike sprint documentation, this document changes rarely.

If another product document conflicts with this one, this document is correct.

---

# Mission

Discovery exists to continuously improve organizational judgment.

It accomplishes this by continuously improving an organization's understanding of itself.

Discovery does not replace executive decision-making.

It improves executive decision-making by helping organizations better understand reality, make better commitments, learn from outcomes, and continuously improve over time.

Every completed organizational cycle should leave both the organization and Discovery more capable than before.

---

# Product Identity

Discovery is an **Executive Operating System**.

Its purpose is to help organizations continuously evolve.

Discovery is not:

- a dashboard,
- a chatbot,
- a reporting platform,
- a document repository,
- a project management system,
- a business intelligence platform,
- an autonomous executive.

Discovery exists to help organizations:

- understand reality,
- manage Executive Work,
- improve executive judgment,
- execute intentionally,
- learn continuously,
- strengthen future organizational performance.

---

# The Organization Is The Product

Discovery is not organized around pages.

Discovery is organized around organizations.

Everything inside Discovery exists to improve one continuously evolving organization.

The organization—not the interface—is the center of the product.

---

# The Operating Model

The Operating Model is Discovery's foundation.

It is Discovery's continuously evolving understanding of how an organization actually operates.

The Operating Model represents:

- organizational reality,
- strategic objectives,
- organizational conditions,
- executive constraints,
- organizational relationships,
- historical decisions,
- executive work,
- organizational learning,
- persistent organizational memory.

Everything Discovery produces originates from the Operating Model.

Including:

- executive understanding,
- recommendations,
- simulations,
- communication,
- confidence,
- executive learning.

Every improvement to the Operating Model should improve every future executive decision.

The Operating Model is Discovery's primary competitive advantage.

---

# Executive Work

Executive Work is the primary organizational object managed inside Discovery.

Executive Work represents intentional organizational change.

Executive Work includes:

- executive decisions,
- executive initiatives,
- Discovery recommendations,
- executive-created work.

Discovery exists to improve Executive Work throughout its lifecycle.

---

# Organizational Lifecycle

Organizations continuously evolve.

Discovery continuously evolves with them.

The canonical lifecycle is:

```text
Organization

↓

Operating Model

↓

Continuous Understanding

↓

Executive Work

↓

Decision Support

↓

Executive Commitment

↓

Execution

↓

Review

↓

Learning

↓

Operating Model Evolution

↓

Repeat
```

Discovery succeeds when organizations repeatedly complete this lifecycle and improve over time.

---

# Executive Judgment

Executive judgment is Discovery's north star.

Discovery improves judgment by helping executives:

- understand more accurately,
- recognize organizational constraints,
- evaluate alternatives,
- understand consequences,
- commit confidently,
- continuously learn.

The objective is not better recommendations.

The objective is better organizations.

---

# Executive Experience

Discovery should always answer the next executive question.

Executives naturally think:

```text
What changed?

↓

Why?

↓

What deserves attention?

↓

What should we do?

↓

What happens if...?

↓

Are we ready?

↓

How is execution progressing?

↓

What did we learn?
```

The application should naturally guide executives through this sequence.

---

# Continuous Understanding

Organizations never stop changing.

Discovery should never stop learning.

New evidence continuously improves:

- organizational understanding,
- confidence,
- recommendations,
- Executive Work,
- future executive judgment.

Executives should never need to "rerun Discovery."

The Operating Model continuously evolves.

---

# Progressive Disclosure

Discovery minimizes executive effort.

Default experience:

```text
Summary

↓

Recommendation

↓

Explanation

↓

Supporting Reasoning
```

Complexity should remain optional.

Executives should never be overwhelmed.

---

# Executive Effort

Discovery should infer as much context as possible.

The default interaction model is:

```text
Discovery Understands

↓

Executive Confirms

↓

Discovery Reasons

↓

Executive Commits

↓

Discovery Learns
```

Executives should spend their time making organizational commitments rather than entering information.

---

# Trust

Trust is Discovery's most valuable product asset.

Discovery earns trust through:

- transparent reasoning,
- explicit uncertainty,
- confidence calibration,
- organizational learning,
- observable improvement over time.

Trust should increase because Discovery continuously proves itself against reality.

---

# Confidence

Confidence exists to improve judgment.

Confidence should always answer:

- Why?
- Compared to what?
- What assumptions matter?
- What evidence is missing?
- What would change this recommendation?

Confidence should never exist to persuade.

It exists to improve executive understanding.

---

# Organizational Memory

Discovery becomes the organization's long-term memory.

It remembers:

- evidence,
- executive work,
- decisions,
- initiatives,
- simulations,
- assumptions,
- outcomes,
- reviews,
- learning.

Organizations become progressively better because Discovery remembers what organizations naturally forget.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Discovery compounds in value through repetition.

```text
Executive Work

↓

Execution

↓

Review

↓

Learning

↓

Operating Model

↓

Better Executive Judgment

↓

Better Executive Work
```

Every completed cycle should strengthen the next.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature should strengthen at least one of:

- organizational understanding,
- Executive Work,
- executive judgment,
- organizational learning,
- Operating Model quality,
- executive trust,
- confidence,
- longitudinal organizational improvement.

If it strengthens none of these, it should not exist.

---

# Product Success

Discovery succeeds when:

- organizations naturally manage Executive Work inside Discovery,
- executives trust Discovery's understanding,
- organizations continuously improve their Operating Model,
- every completed Executive Work item improves future executive judgment,
- Discovery demonstrably becomes better as organizations evolve.

Discovery's success is measured not by the quality of individual recommendations, but by the long-term improvement of organizational judgment.
