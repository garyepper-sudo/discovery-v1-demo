# Discovery Product Canon

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines the canonical product philosophy of Discovery.

Every product decision, UX decision, workflow, feature, engineering investment, and customer validation effort should reinforce this philosophy.

This document answers one question:

> **What is Discovery?**

If another product document contradicts this one, this document is correct.

---

# Mission

Discovery exists to continuously improve the quality of executive judgment.

Discovery does not replace executive decision-making.

Discovery improves it.

It accomplishes this by building and continuously improving an Operating Model that understands how an organization actually works.

---

# Product Vision

Organizations make thousands of strategic decisions and initiatives.

Very few organizations systematically improve how they perform that work.

Discovery becomes the Executive Operating System organizations naturally use to:

- understand their organization,
- evaluate important Executive Work,
- simulate alternatives,
- commit decisions,
- track execution,
- review outcomes,
- continuously improve future judgment.

Every completed Executive Work item should improve the Operating Model.

Every improvement to the Operating Model should improve future Executive Work.

Over time, Discovery becomes progressively better at helping organizations make important decisions because its Operating Model continuously learns from organizational reality.

---

# Product Philosophy

Discovery is not:

- a dashboard,
- a chatbot,
- a document repository,
- a reporting platform,
- a business intelligence tool,
- a project management system.

Discovery is an **Executive Operating System**.

Its purpose is to continuously improve executive judgment through a living Operating Model.

---

# The Operating Model

The Operating Model is Discovery's foundation.

It is the continuously evolving cognitive representation of how an organization operates.

Everything Discovery produces originates from the Operating Model.

Including:

- organizational understanding,
- recommendations,
- simulations,
- confidence,
- initiatives,
- executive decisions,
- execution tracking,
- organizational learning.

The Operating Model is Discovery's competitive advantage.

The product should continuously reinforce its value.

Every improvement to the Operating Model should improve every future recommendation, simulation, explanation, and executive decision.

---

# Executive Work

Discovery organizes itself around Executive Work.

Executive Work includes:

- Discovery-generated recommendations,
- Discovery-generated initiatives,
- executive-created decisions,
- executive-created initiatives.

Discovery reasons about all Executive Work using the same Operating Model.

Origin becomes irrelevant once work enters Discovery.

---

# Executive Learning Cycle

Every Executive Work item contributes to a continuous organizational learning cycle.

```text
Executive Work

↓

Executive Review

↓

Executive Learning

↓

Operating Model Improvement

↓

Persistent Organizational Memory

↓

Longitudinal Executive Judgment
```

This cycle defines Discovery's long-term value.

Every feature should strengthen one or more stages of this cycle.

---

# The Executive Conversation

Discovery should feel like a trusted executive advisor.

Executives naturally ask questions in a predictable order.

Discovery should answer them in the same order.

## 1. What should I do?

Provide one clear recommendation.

---

## 2. Why?

Explain Discovery's reasoning.

Surface:

- supporting evidence,
- mechanisms,
- theories,
- decisive advantages.

---

## 3. What else could I do?

Present alternative strategies.

Explain tradeoffs.

Compare expected outcomes.

---

## 4. What could make this wrong?

Expose:

- assumptions,
- uncertainty,
- confidence limiters,
- evidence that could change the recommendation,
- important risks.

---

## 5. What happens if...?

Simulate alternative futures.

Help executives explore consequences before committing.

---

## 6. What happened?

Track execution.

Review outcomes.

Compare expectations with reality.

---

## 7. What did we learn?

Improve the Operating Model.

Strengthen Persistent Organizational Memory.

Improve future executive judgment.

---

# Progressive Disclosure

Discovery minimizes reading.

Executives should never be overwhelmed.

Information should appear progressively.

Level 1

Executive recommendation.

Level 2

Reasoning.

Level 3

Alternatives.

Level 4

Challenge.

Level 5

Supporting evidence.

Complexity should always remain optional.

---

# Discovery's Role

Discovery never owns executive decisions.

Executives own commitments.

Discovery owns:

- organizational understanding,
- reasoning,
- simulation,
- confidence,
- tracking,
- learning,
- Operating Model evolution.

Discovery should always feel like a trusted executive thought partner.

Never an automated decision maker.

---

# Trust

Trust is Discovery's most valuable feature.

Discovery earns trust by:

- explaining reasoning,
- exposing uncertainty,
- recommending validation,
- admitting limitations,
- comparing predictions with reality,
- continuously learning from outcomes.

Discovery should never imply certainty where uncertainty exists.

---

# Confidence

Confidence belongs to Executive Work.

Confidence should always answer:

- Why is confidence high?
- Why is confidence limited?
- Which assumptions matter most?
- What evidence would improve confidence?
- How much improvement is expected?

Confidence should improve executive judgment.

Not persuade executives to agree.

---

# Observable Cognition

Discovery should never pretend to think.

Instead, Discovery should expose authentic cognition occurring inside the Operating Model.

Examples include:

- building organizational understanding,
- updating mechanisms,
- strengthening theories,
- evaluating interventions,
- propagating organizational effects,
- comparing strategies,
- optimizing recommendations,
- calibrating confidence,
- learning from executive outcomes.

Executives should observe real reasoning.

Not artificial loading animations.

---

# Operating Model Visibility

The Operating Model should always remain visible throughout the product.

Executives should continuously understand:

Everything Discovery knows originates from the Operating Model.

Whenever the Operating Model improves, Discovery should explain:

- what improved,
- why it improved,
- how recommendations become stronger,
- how confidence increases,
- how future executive judgment benefits.

Executives should naturally understand the relationship:

```text
Better Organizational Information

↓

Better Operating Model

↓

Better Understanding

↓

Better Recommendations

↓

Better Executive Judgment

↓

Better Organizational Outcomes
```

---

# Persistent Organizational Memory

Discovery becomes the organization's long-term memory for Executive Work.

Discovery remembers:

- why work was started,
- what assumptions existed,
- what was recommended,
- what simulations predicted,
- what was committed,
- what actually happened,
- what the organization learned.

Persistent Organizational Memory allows every future Executive Work item to benefit from previous organizational experience.

Organizations become progressively better decision-makers because Discovery remembers what organizations forget.

---

# Product Flywheel

Discovery should become more valuable every time it is used.

```text
Executive Work

↓

Executive Review

↓

Executive Learning

↓

Operating Model Improvement

↓

Persistent Organizational Memory

↓

Longitudinal Executive Judgment

↓

Higher-Quality Executive Work
```

The product compounds through organizational learning rather than manual configuration.

Every cycle improves the next cycle.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature should improve one or more of:

- Operating Model quality,
- organizational understanding,
- executive confidence,
- executive judgment,
- executive decision quality,
- organizational learning.

Features that do not improve one of these outcomes should not be built.

---

# Product Success

Discovery succeeds when executive teams naturally begin important Executive Work inside Discovery.

Discovery succeeds when organizations voluntarily strengthen the Operating Model because they understand its value.

Discovery succeeds when every Executive Review improves future executive judgment.

Discovery succeeds when Persistent Organizational Memory becomes a strategic organizational asset.

Discovery succeeds when the Operating Model becomes the trusted organizational brain behind every important executive decision.

That is the purpose of Discovery.
