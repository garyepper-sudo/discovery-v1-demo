# Organization Experience Canon

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This document defines the canonical executive experience of Discovery.

It answers one question:

> **How should an executive experience the Organization Model?**

Every screen, workflow, interaction, visualization, onboarding decision, navigation decision, and future product investment should reinforce this experience.

If another UX specification contradicts this document, this document is correct.

---

# Core Principle

**The Organization Model is the product.**

Discovery is not a collection of tools.

It is not:

- a dashboard,
- a chatbot,
- a document repository,
- a reporting platform,
- or a project management system.

Discovery exists to help executives continuously develop, steward, and benefit from a living Organization Model.

Everything else is simply a different interaction with that model.

---

# Product Architecture

Discovery consists of one underlying product:

> **The Organization Model**

Executives interact with that model through four perspectives.

```
Organization Model

├── Your Organization
│   Steward the model.
│
├── Decisions
│   Act using the model.
│
├── Research
│   Expand the model.
│
└── Ask
    Converse with the model.
```

Navigation changes perspective.

It does not change products.

---

# Executive Mental Model

Executives should never feel like they are configuring software.

They should feel like they are teaching Discovery how their organization actually works.

Over time Discovery earns trust by demonstrating an increasingly accurate understanding of organizational reality.

The executive's role is not to maintain software.

The executive's role is to cultivate the Organization Model.

---

# The Organization Model

The Organization Model is Discovery's continuously evolving understanding of an organization.

It contains:

- organizational objectives,
- organizational structure,
- relationships,
- operating mechanisms,
- organizational beliefs,
- organizational conditions,
- executive decisions,
- executive work,
- historical learning,
- organizational memory.

Everything Discovery produces originates from this model.

Including:

- Today's Story
- Recommendations
- Executive Decisions
- Simulations
- Organizational Learning
- Research
- Executive Answers

Nothing exists independently of the Organization Model.

---

# Executive Experience

Discovery should always answer four questions.

## 1. What does Discovery currently understand?

Executives should immediately understand Discovery's current understanding of the organization.

This becomes Today's Story.

---

## 2. Why does Discovery believe that?

Executives should understand the reasoning behind Discovery's understanding.

Confidence, supporting evidence, and explanation should always be available through progressive disclosure.

---

## 3. What changed?

Executives should immediately understand how Discovery's understanding has evolved since their previous visit.

Discovery is a living model.

Its evolution should be visible.

---

## 4. What would most improve the Organization Model?

Discovery should continuously identify the next highest-value organizational input.

Every request for additional information must justify itself by explaining how it will improve organizational understanding.

---

# Information Hierarchy

Every Organization experience should prioritize information in this order.

```
Today's Story

↓

Organization Model

↓

What Changed

↓

Strengthen Understanding
```

Nothing should compete with Today's Story as the primary organizational insight.

---

# The Reward Loop

Discovery earns organizational knowledge.

It never demands it.

Every interaction follows this loop.

```
Teach

↓

Discovery Learns

↓

Discovery Produces Value

↓

Executive Validates or Challenges

↓

Organization Model Improves

↓

Discovery Earns the Next Request
```

Every request for additional information must follow visible value.

Executives should never feel like they are completing forms.

---

# Organization Model Maturity

The Organization page never changes.

Only the maturity of the Organization Model changes.

Canonical maturity states:

```
EMPTY

↓

EMERGING

↓

DEVELOPING

↓

ACTIVE

↓

EVOLVING
```

Each maturity state exposes additional capabilities while preserving the same overall experience.

There is no separate onboarding workflow.

---

# Onboarding Philosophy

Discovery has no onboarding wizard.

The first visit is simply the earliest stage of the Organization Model.

Instead of asking users to complete setup, Discovery begins learning immediately.

For example:

> Understanding is just beginning.

> The first thing I'd like to understand is...

> What is the most important initiative your organization is working on?

Future visits use the same page.

Only the model has evolved.

---

# Product Principles

## Story before metrics.

Executives should first understand organizational reality.

Metrics support the story.

They do not replace it.

---

## Understanding before action.

Recommendations are only meaningful after organizational understanding exists.

Every decision should naturally emerge from Discovery's understanding.

---

## Stewardship before configuration.

Executives improve Discovery's understanding.

They do not configure software.

---

## Progressive disclosure.

Executives should see concise organizational understanding first.

Evidence, confidence, reasoning, and supporting details appear only when requested.

---

## Reward before request.

Discovery should demonstrate value before requesting additional organizational knowledge.

Every question should feel earned.

---

## Continuous evolution.

Discovery should visibly become more intelligent over time.

Executives should feel like they are investing in a living organizational asset.

---

# Navigation Philosophy

Navigation changes perspective.

It does not change context.

Every page should operate on the same Organization Model.

There should never be multiple competing organizational realities.

---

# Decisions

Decisions represent action informed by the Organization Model.

They are consequences of organizational understanding.

They are not independent workflows.

---

# Research

Research expands organizational understanding.

Research exists to improve the Organization Model.

---

# Ask

Ask enables natural conversation with the Organization Model.

Executives are not chatting with an AI.

They are interrogating Discovery's current understanding of their organization.

---

# Success

Discovery succeeds when executives no longer think about using Discovery.

Instead they think about improving their Organization Model.

Every interaction should reinforce one belief:

> The better Discovery understands my organization, the better it helps me lead it.
