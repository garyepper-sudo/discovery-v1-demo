# Atlas Simulation Architecture

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

Atlas is Discovery's **Organizational Validation and Simulation Engine**.

Its purpose is to continuously validate that Discovery develops accurate, explainable, adaptive, and self-improving organizational understanding.

Atlas does not merely benchmark output quality.

Atlas validates Discovery's complete Cognitive Operating System by repeatedly placing Discovery inside evolving organizational environments and measuring whether Discovery:

* correctly understands the organization,
* forms coherent executive theories,
* predicts future organizational behavior,
* evaluates prediction accuracy,
* learns from prediction outcomes,
* revises beliefs appropriately,
* calibrates confidence,
* improves future understanding,
* and becomes a better organizational learner over time.

Atlas is therefore the canonical validation environment for Discovery's intelligence.

---

# Design Principles

Atlas validates **adaptive learning**, not merely reasoning.

Producing a correct answer once is insufficient.

Discovery should demonstrate that its understanding becomes stronger as organizational evidence accumulates.

Every simulation should answer:

* What does Discovery currently believe?
* Why does Discovery believe it?
* What does Discovery predict will happen next?
* What evidence would change Discovery's mind?
* What actually happened?
* Was Discovery correct?
* What did Discovery learn?
* How should Discovery adapt?

---

Every Atlas simulation executes the same longitudinal cognitive loop.

```text
Organization State

Organizational Evidence

↓

Discovery Investigation

↓

Organizational Understanding

↓

Executive Assessment

↓

Organizational Predictions

↓

Executive Intervention (optional)

↓

↓

↓

New Organizational Evidence

↓

Prediction Evaluation

↓

Prediction Learning Events
```

Atlas exists to repeatedly execute this loop until Discovery demonstrates stable, measurable organizational learning.

# Simulation Phases

## Phase 1 — Observe

Atlas generates the organizational evidence available during the current period.

Examples include:

* executive interviews
* customer feedback
* engineering updates
* operational metrics
* organizational events
* strategic decisions

Discovery reasons only from evidence available during the current period.

Future evidence is never visible.

---

## Phase 2 — Understand

Discovery executes its complete canonical reasoning pipeline.

Evidence becomes:

* observations
* mechanisms
* beliefs
* theories
* organizational conditions
* executive assessment
* executive understanding

This establishes Discovery's current organizational understanding.

---

## Phase 3 — Predict

Discovery predicts how the organization is likely to evolve.

Predictions include:

* expected condition changes
* downstream effects
* organizational propagation
* executive risks
* recommended executive actions
* confidence
* uncertainty
* falsifying evidence

Predictions must be committed before future evidence becomes available.

---

## Phase 4 — Simulate

Atlas advances the organization forward in time.

Simulation may include:

* natural organizational evolution
* executive interventions
* environmental changes
* staffing changes
* market conditions
* strategic decisions
* unexpected organizational events

Simulation produces the next organizational state.

---

## Phase 5 — Evaluate

Atlas compares previous predictions against newly observed organizational conditions.

Each prediction is evaluated as:

* Confirmed
* Partially Confirmed
* Not Confirmed
* Inconclusive

Evaluation determines:

* prediction accuracy,
* confidence calibration,
* supporting evidence,
* contradictory evidence,
* and learning signals.

Prediction Evaluation is the canonical producer of organizational forecasting performance.

---

## Phase 6 — Learn

Prediction evaluations become canonical learning events.

Learning now occurs through three independent cognitive streams:

* Belief Revision
* Theory Evolution
* Prediction Learning

These streams are integrated into the Organizational Learning Profile.

Discovery therefore learns not only from changing beliefs, but from the measured accuracy of its own predictions.

---

# Canonical Learning Pipeline

Adaptive learning follows the canonical architecture:

```text
Prediction Evaluation

↓

Prediction Learning Events

↓

Prediction Learning Summary

↓

Organizational Learning Profile

↓

Simulation

↓

Future Understanding
```

Each layer owns a distinct cognitive responsibility.

No downstream system consumes raw prediction evaluations directly.

---

# Longitudinal Simulation

Atlas is fundamentally longitudinal.

Each simulated period inherits the complete organizational runtime from the previous period.

Example:

```text
Week 1

↓

Week 2

↓

Week 3

↓

Month 2

↓

Quarter 2

↓

Year 2
```

Discovery accumulates understanding rather than restarting from scratch.

---

# Executive Interventions

Atlas may introduce executive decisions between simulation periods.

Examples include:

* organizational restructuring
* hiring freeze
* leadership departure
* strategic reprioritization
* operating model redesign
* documentation initiatives
* clarified decision rights

Discovery should recognize when executive intervention changes the expected trajectory.

Successful intervention should not automatically invalidate otherwise sound reasoning.

---

# Simulation Objectives

Atlas evaluates whether Discovery improves across time.

Desired behavior includes:

* stronger organizational understanding
* improved prediction accuracy
* improved forecast reliability
* better confidence calibration
* adaptive belief revision
* improved investigation quality
* improved executive recommendations
* progressively stronger organizational learning

Discovery should become measurably better with experience.

# Sources of Organizational Change

Future organizational states may evolve because of:

* executive intervention
* organizational learning
* market pressure
* customer behavior
* staffing changes
* operational failures
* execution improvements
* random organizational events

Atlas should never become completely deterministic.

Unexpected events are necessary to validate adaptive reasoning.

---

# Canonical Success Criteria

Discovery succeeds when it consistently demonstrates:

* accurate organizational understanding,
* explainable reasoning,
* calibrated confidence,
* reliable predictions,
* measurable forecast improvement,
* adaptive learning,
* progressively stronger executive recommendations,
* and continuously improving organizational understanding.

Atlas validates not only what Discovery understands today, but whether Discovery becomes a more capable organizational intelligence system over time.
