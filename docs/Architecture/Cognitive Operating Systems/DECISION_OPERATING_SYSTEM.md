# Decision Operating System

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

The Decision Operating System transforms organizational understanding into executive decisions.

It determines **what leadership should decide** before determining **how the organization should optimize that decision**.

The Decision Operating System identifies the decision.

The Optimization Operating System determines the best strategy for executing it.

---

# Executive Question

The Decision Operating System answers:

> **What decision does leadership need to make?**

Not:

- What is happening?
- Why is it happening?
- What is the best strategy?

Those belong to other Operating Systems.

Decision identifies the executive problem.

Optimization solves it.

# Inputs

The Decision Operating System consumes:

- Organizational Understanding
- Organizational State
- Organizational Conditions
- Organizational Concepts
- Organizational Beliefs
- Organizational Theories
- Executive Assessments
- Predictions
- Simulation Insights (optional)

Decision does not perform simulation.

Decision does not optimize.

---

# Outputs

Decision produces:

- Executive Decision
- Executive Objective
- Executive Success Metrics
- Executive Constraints
- Executive Questions
- Candidate Intervention Domains

---

# Core Philosophy

Organizations do not implement recommendations.

Organizations make decisions.

Recommendations exist to support decisions.

The Decision Operating System exists to determine **which decision matters most.**

# CAP-DEC-001

## Executive Opportunity Detection

Question

> What requires executive attention?

Inputs

- Organizational Understanding
- Organizational Conditions
- Organizational State

Outputs

- Executive Opportunities

Responsibilities

- identify executive leverage,
- identify important organizational risks,
- identify unresolved organizational tensions,
- identify high-impact opportunities.

---

# CAP-DEC-002

## Decision Framing

Question

> What decision is actually being made?

Example

Organizational Understanding

```
Execution is slowing.
```

Decision

```
Improve organizational execution.
```

Decision framing converts diagnosis into executive intent.

---

# CAP-DEC-003

## Objective Definition

Question

> What outcome does leadership want?

Examples

- Improve execution
- Reduce organizational risk
- Increase coordination
- Improve strategic alignment

Objectives describe desired organizational change.

---

# CAP-DEC-004

## Success Metric Definition

Question

> How will Discovery know the decision succeeded?

Examples

- Execution Capacity
- Decision Flow
- Customer Satisfaction
- Time to Delivery

Every success metric references a canonical organizational condition.

---

# CAP-DEC-005

## Constraint Identification

Question

> What must not happen?

Examples

- Budget Neutral
- No Layoffs
- Regulatory Compliance
- Near-term Improvement
- Preserve Customer Experience

Constraints are not optimization.

Constraints describe executive boundaries.

---

# CAP-DEC-006

## Decision Confidence

Question

> How well does Discovery understand this decision?

Decision confidence considers:

- organizational understanding,
- evidence quality,
- prediction confidence,
- unresolved uncertainty,
- executive assumptions.

# Executive Decision Object

The Executive Decision contains:

- objective,
- rationale,
- success metrics,
- constraints,
- assumptions,
- open questions,
- confidence,
- target organizational conditions.

The Executive Decision is the canonical output of this Operating System.

---

# Decision Quality

A high-quality decision:

- addresses an important organizational problem,
- has measurable success,
- respects executive constraints,
- possesses sufficient evidence,
- clearly defines desired outcomes.

---

# Explainability

Every Executive Decision must explain:

- why this decision matters,
- why it matters now,
- what success looks like,
- what constraints exist,
- what assumptions remain,
- what uncertainty still exists.

Decision framing must always be explainable.

---

# Relationship to Optimization

Decision answers:

> What should leadership decide?

Optimization answers:

> Given that decision, what is the best strategy?

These Operating Systems are intentionally separate.

---

# Relationship to Simulation

Simulation evaluates strategies.

Decision determines whether those strategies should be considered.

Simulation supports Decision.

Simulation does not replace Decision.

---

# Relationship to Learning

Completed decisions become organizational memory.

Discovery learns:

- decision quality,
- decision outcomes,
- prediction accuracy,
- executive preferences,
- recurring decision patterns.

Future decisions improve because previous decisions become organizational knowledge.

---

# Product Role

The Decision Operating System powers:

- Executive Recommendations
- Executive Briefings
- Decision Records
- Optimization
- Board Reports

Executives interact primarily with the Decision Operating System.

Optimization remains behind the scenes.

---

# Definition of Success

The Decision Operating System succeeds when Discovery can:

- identify the most important executive decision,
- frame that decision clearly,
- define measurable success,
- identify meaningful constraints,
- explain why the decision matters,
- prepare the decision for optimization.

Decision identifies **what should be decided**.

Optimization determines **how to achieve it.**
