# Discovery Understanding Operating System

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

The Understanding Operating System enables Discovery to transform organizational evidence into durable explanations of how the organization currently works.

Its purpose is to answer:

> **What does Discovery currently understand about the organization, and why?**

Understanding is the core interpretive domain of Discovery.

It converts observed organizational reality into increasingly integrated explanations, beliefs, theories, conditions, state, and executive understanding.

---

# Cognitive Responsibility

The Understanding Operating System is responsible for:

- interpreting organizational evidence,
- inferring organizational mechanisms,
- forming organizational beliefs,
- constructing organizational theories,
- identifying organizational conditions,
- synthesizing organizational state,
- and producing canonical Organizational Understanding.

It does not merely summarize evidence.

It determines what the evidence means.

# Canonical Producer Rule

Every cognitive object in the Understanding Operating System must have exactly one canonical producer.

Examples:

```text
Organizational Mechanism
→ Mechanism Engine

Organizational Belief
→ Belief Engine

Organizational Concept
→ Organizational Concept Formation

Organizational Theory
→ Theory Engine

Organizational Condition
→ Condition Engine

Organizational State
→ Organizational State Engine

Organizational Understanding
→ Executive Assessment
```

Multiple canonical producers for the same object indicate an architectural defect.

---

---

# Confidence Model

Confidence belongs to the transformation that produces the cognitive object.

Examples:

- mechanism confidence belongs to mechanism inference,
- belief confidence belongs to belief formation,
- theory confidence belongs to theory formation,
- condition confidence belongs to condition inference,
- Organizational Understanding confidence belongs to Executive Assessment.

Confidence should reflect:

- evidence breadth,
- evidence quality,
- causal support,
- longitudinal stability,
- contradiction level,
- competing explanations,
- and explanatory power.

Confidence must never be treated as certainty.

# Runtime Integration

Understanding is not complete until it persists.

The runtime should preserve:

- current understandings,
- mechanisms,
- beliefs,
- concepts,
- theories,
- conditions,
- state,
- Executive Assessment,
- provenance,
- confidence,
- and longitudinal evolution.

Each investigation should evolve the same organizational understanding rather than begin from zero.

---

# Executive Projection

The Executive Projection must expose understanding without recreating it.

The projection may transform:

- field names,
- ordering,
- formatting,
- and presentation priority.

It must not infer:

- new beliefs,
- new theories,
- new conditions,
- new confidence,
- or new executive conclusions.

The Executive Experience remains presentation-only.

# Architectural Principles

The Understanding Operating System should always:

1. Transform evidence into explanation.
2. Preserve causal provenance.
3. Maintain exactly one canonical producer per object.
4. Prefer durable cognition over temporary summaries.
5. Distinguish current diagnosis from future prediction.
6. Consider competing explanations.
7. Preserve contradictory evidence.
8. Calibrate confidence explicitly.
9. Persist understanding across investigations.
10. Project cognition without recreating it.
11. Improve existing transformations before adding new engines.
12. Optimize for executive usefulness without sacrificing reasoning integrity.

# Definition of Success

The Understanding Operating System is successful when Discovery can:

- explain what is happening,
- explain how it is happening,
- explain why it is happening,
- distinguish strong explanations from weak ones,
- identify what remains unknown,
- state what would change its mind,
- preserve understanding through time,
- and provide executives with a coherent, traceable, confidence-calibrated view of the organization.

The output should become more accurate, integrated, and useful as organizational experience accumulates.
