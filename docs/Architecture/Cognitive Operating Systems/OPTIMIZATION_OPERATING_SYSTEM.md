# Optimization Operating System

**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

The Optimization Operating System determines the best organizational strategy for achieving an executive objective.

Optimization does not create organizational understanding.

Optimization does not perform simulation.

Optimization consumes organizational understanding, executive intent, and simulated organizational futures to determine the optimal executive recommendation.

Optimization answers:

> **Given the organization's objectives and constraints, what is the best available course of action?**

---

# Executive Question

Optimization exists to answer a single executive question.

> **What should we do?**

Not:

- What is happening?
- Why is it happening?
- What might happen?

Those questions belong to other Operating Systems.

Optimization determines which organizational future is most desirable.

---

Simulation is an internal capability used by Optimization.

---

# Inputs

Optimization consumes:

- Executive Decision
- Organizational Understanding
- Organizational State
- Organizational Conditions
- Organizational Beliefs
- Organizational Concepts
- Organizational Theories
- Organizational Causal Model
- Organizational Simulation Results

Optimization never creates new organizational understanding.

It evaluates possible organizational futures.

---

# Outputs

Optimization produces:

- Executive Optimization Objective
- Candidate Strategies
- Constraint Evaluation
- Strategy Comparison
- Optimization Scores
- Executive Recommendation

---

# Core Philosophy

Discovery does not optimize recommendations.

Discovery optimizes organizational futures.

Recommendations are simply the highest-ranked organizational future.

---

# CAP-OPT-001

## Optimization Variable Selection

Question:

> Which organizational variables matter for this executive decision?

Inputs:

- Executive Decision
- Organizational Conditions

Outputs:

- Optimization Variables

Responsibilities:

- identify primary variables,
- identify secondary variables,
- identify protected variables,
- assign variable weights,
- explain variable selection.

This capability performs no optimization.

---

# CAP-OPT-002

## Executive Optimization Objective Synthesis

Question:

> What optimization problem should Discovery solve?

Inputs:

- Executive Decision
- Optimization Variables
- Success Metrics
- Executive Constraints

Outputs:

- Executive Optimization Objective

Responsibilities:

- translate executive objectives,
- translate success metrics,
- translate constraints,
- define optimization preferences,
- define optimization strategy.

---

# CAP-OPT-003

## Candidate Strategy Generation

Question:

> What organizational strategies could achieve the objective?

Inputs:

- Executive Optimization Objective

Outputs:

- Candidate Intervention Options

Responsibilities:

- generate multiple strategies,
- define assumptions,
- identify risks,
- identify required evidence.

---

# CAP-OPT-004

## Constraint Evaluation

Question:

> Which strategies are feasible?

Inputs:

- Executive Optimization Objective
- Candidate Strategies

Outputs:

- Constraint Evaluations

Constraint states:

- Satisfied
- Violated
- Requires Simulation
- Insufficient Evidence

Optimization never assumes a constraint is satisfied without evidence.

---

# CAP-OPT-005

## Organizational Simulation

Question:

> What future organizational state results from each strategy?

Inputs:

- Candidate Strategy
- Organizational Causal Model

Outputs:

- Simulated Organizational Future

Simulation predicts:

- organizational variables,
- conditions,
- beliefs,
- executive assessment,
- future understanding.

Simulation evaluates futures.

Optimization selects among them.

---

# CAP-OPT-006

## Future Comparison

Question:

> How do the candidate futures differ?

Inputs:

- Simulated Futures

Outputs:

- Variable Comparisons
- Tradeoffs
- Confidence Differences

Comparison preserves:

- before values,
- after values,
- variable movement,
- confidence,
- explanation.

---

# CAP-OPT-007

## Organizational Optimization

Question:

> Which future best satisfies executive objectives?

Optimization considers:

- objective alignment,
- success metric attainment,
- organizational variable movement,
- constraint satisfaction,
- organizational risk,
- confidence,
- executive preferences,
- tradeoffs.

Optimization produces an overall Optimization Score.

---

# CAP-OPT-008

## Executive Recommendation

Question:

> Which strategy should leadership implement?

Outputs include:

- recommended strategy,
- confidence,
- explanation,
- tradeoffs,
- remaining risks,
- assumptions,
- evidence that could change the recommendation.

Recommendations must always be explainable.

---

# Organizational Variables

Optimization operates over canonical organizational variables.

Examples include:

- Decision Flow
- Execution Capacity
- Coordination System
- Knowledge Continuity
- Learning System
- Strategic Alignment
- Leadership Dependency
- Operating Model

Each variable possesses:

- strength,
- confidence,
- status,
- trend,
- relationships,
- executive importance.

---

# Variable Roles

Variables are classified as:

Primary

Variables directly identified by the Executive Decision.

Secondary

Variables connected through organizational causality.

Protected

Variables that should not deteriorate while pursuing the objective.

---

# Variable Weights

Version 1 weighting combines:

- Executive Priority
- Organizational Priority
- Network Influence

Future versions may additionally consider:

- historical optimization performance,
- executive preferences,
- organizational maturity,
- longitudinal learning.

---

# Success Metrics

Success Metrics define measurable executive outcomes.

Every Success Metric references a canonical organizational variable.

Optimization evaluates:

- baseline,
- projected value,
- target,
- attainment.

---

# Constraints

Constraints define unacceptable organizational futures.

Examples:

- Budget
- Time
- Capacity
- People
- Technology
- Regulatory
- Strategic

Constraints are translated before optimization.

Constraint evaluation occurs separately.

---

# Preferences

Preferences influence optimization without eliminating strategies.

Examples:

- maximize confidence,
- minimize disruption,
- minimize implementation effort,
- maximize speed.

Preferences guide optimization after hard constraints.

---

# Tradeoffs

Optimization explicitly exposes tradeoffs.

Example:

```
Execution Capacity

↑

Decision Flow

↑

Knowledge Continuity

↓

Leadership Dependency

↓

```

Discovery never hides negative consequences.

---

# Explainability

Every recommendation must explain:

- why it won,
- what improved,
- what worsened,
- which objectives were achieved,
- which constraints were satisfied,
- what evidence could change the recommendation.

Optimization is fully explainable.

---

# Learning

Optimization improves through executive outcomes.

Immediate learning:

- recommendation accepted,
- recommendation rejected,
- assumptions challenged,
- constraint corrections.

Long-term learning:

- implementation success,
- prediction accuracy,
- optimization quality,
- executive satisfaction.

Every completed decision improves future optimization.

---

# Product Role

Optimization powers:

- Executive Recommendation
- Recommendation Challenge
- Simulation
- Board Reports
- Decision Records
- Organizational Learning

Executives never interact directly with Optimization.

They interact with recommendations generated by it.

---

# Definition of Success

The Optimization Operating System succeeds when Discovery can:

- translate executive intent into an optimization problem,
- identify the correct organizational variables,
- evaluate multiple organizational futures,
- select the strongest available strategy,
- explain why it won,
- expose remaining tradeoffs,
- and improve future decisions through learning.

Discovery does not optimize recommendations.

Discovery optimizes **organizational futures**.
