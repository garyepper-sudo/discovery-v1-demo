# MENTAL_MODELS.md

# Discovery Cognitive Architecture

## Architectural Milestone

**Mental Models**

---

# Purpose

Memory preserves organizational experience.

Understanding explains organizational experience.

**Mental Models explain why organizational experience repeatedly occurs.**

A Mental Model is Discovery's highest-level cognitive representation of an organization.

It is not a fact.

It is not an observation.

It is not a belief.

It is not a theme.

It is not an individual understanding.

It is an enduring explanation that organizes many understandings into one coherent view of how the organization works.

---

# Discovery North Star

Discovery continuously transforms organizational experience into enduring organizational understanding.

Mental Models represent the most compressed and durable form of that understanding.

They are Discovery's evolving explanation of organizational reality.

---

---

# Why Mental Models Exist

Organizations generate enormous amounts of information.

Without compression, Discovery accumulates thousands of disconnected observations.

Real organizational understanding requires reducing many observations into a small number of stable explanations.

Mental Models provide this compression.

They answer:

> **"What enduring explanation best accounts for everything Discovery has learned?"**

---

# Characteristics

A Mental Model should:

- explain many understandings
- survive multiple investigations
- strengthen through repeated evidence
- weaken when contradictory evidence appears
- evolve instead of being replaced
- become increasingly stable over time

Unlike observations, Mental Models should change slowly.

---

# Example

### Observations

- Hiring delays continue.
- Open engineering positions remain.
- Delivery milestones slipped.
- Engineers are overloaded.

↓

### Beliefs

Hiring capacity may be limiting execution.

↓

### Understanding

Engineering staffing appears to constrain delivery performance.

↓

### Mental Model

**Engineering capacity constrains organizational execution.**

The Mental Model explains every observation above.

# Mental Model Evolution

Mental Models are living cognitive structures.

They continuously evolve.

Possible transitions include:

## Created

Discovery identifies a new explanatory model.

---

## Strengthened

Additional investigations support the explanation.

Confidence increases.

Support increases.

---

## Refined

Discovery discovers a better explanation while preserving continuity.

The explanation changes without losing its identity.

Example:

Before

Engineering hiring slows execution.

After

Engineering capacity limits organizational execution.

The explanation becomes broader and more accurate.

---

## Weakened

Contradictory evidence reduces confidence.

The model remains but becomes less certain.

---

## Split

Discovery realizes one Mental Model actually represents two independent explanations.

Example

"Execution problems"

↓

becomes

- Hiring Capacity
- Manufacturing Constraints

---

## Merged

Discovery recognizes two Mental Models describe the same organizational reality.

Example

Recruiting Problems

+

Engineering Capacity

↓

Engineering Capacity Limits Execution

---

## Retired

Repeated contradictory evidence causes the explanation to disappear.

Retirement should be rare.

Understanding should evolve whenever possible.

---

# Compression

Compression is a primary cognitive objective.

The goal is not to maximize remembered information.

The goal is to maximize explanatory power.

An ideal Mental Model explains many observations.

As Discovery matures:

- observations increase
- understandings increase
- Mental Models increase slowly

The number of Mental Models should grow much more slowly than observations.

---

# Stability

Mental Models become increasingly stable.

Example

Observation

Support = 1

↓

Belief

Support = 2

↓

Understanding

Support = 6

↓

Mental Model

Support = 27

Confidence = 94%

Stable

A stable Mental Model represents enduring organizational knowledge.

---

# Executive Utility

Executives rarely need hundreds of observations.

They need a small number of reliable explanations.

Mental Models therefore become Discovery's primary executive language.

Examples include:

- Engineering capacity limits execution.
- Decision making is highly centralized.
- Customer demand exceeds operational capability.
- Product quality improves faster than delivery speed.
- Knowledge is concentrated within a small number of employees.

These explain organizational behavior rather than individual events.

---

# Relationship to Organizational Understanding State

The Organizational Understanding State becomes the persistent collection of Mental Models.

Memory stores organizational experience.

Mental Models organize organizational understanding.

Executive reasoning should operate primarily over Mental Models rather than raw observations.

---

## Continuity

---

# Architectural Principle

Discovery should remember fewer things while understanding more.

Success is measured not by the amount of information stored, but by the quality of the explanations that organize that information.

The highest form of organizational understanding is not a collection of facts.

It is a small number of evolving Mental Models that accurately explain how the organization behaves.
