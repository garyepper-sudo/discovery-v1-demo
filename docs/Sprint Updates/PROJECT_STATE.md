# Current Milestone

**Milestone:** Executive Operating System MVP

# Current Development Phase

**Executive Product Refinement**

Discovery has completed the first production implementation of its Executive Cognitive Operating System.

The architecture is considered stable.

The Executive Decision lifecycle is now implemented end-to-end.

Current development is no longer focused on expanding reasoning capabilities.

Current development focuses on exposing existing cognition through a production-quality Executive Operating System.

Future architectural expansion should occur only when benchmark evidence demonstrates a genuine reasoning gap.

---

# Product Identity

Discovery is an **Executive Operating System**.

Its purpose is to continuously improve executive judgment.

Discovery does not replace executive decision-making.

It improves executive decision-making through a continuously evolving **Operating Model**.

Discovery is not:

- a dashboard,
- a chatbot,
- a reporting platform,
- a document repository,
- a project management system,
- a business intelligence platform,
- an autonomous executive.

Discovery is an Executive Operating System powered by a living Operating Model.

---

# The Operating Model

The Operating Model remains Discovery's central product asset.

It represents Discovery's continuously evolving understanding of:

- organizational reality,
- organizational conditions,
- executive constraints,
- strategic objectives,
- organizational dependencies,
- executive decisions,
- initiatives,
- historical outcomes,
- executive learning,
- persistent organizational memory.

Everything Discovery produces originates from the Operating Model.

Including:

- executive understanding,
- recommendations,
- simulations,
- confidence,
- executive communication,
- decision support,
- organizational learning.

Every product improvement should either:

- strengthen the Operating Model, or
- expose it more effectively.

# Decision Definition

Decision Definition should function as **Executive Intent Confirmation**.

Discovery already understands:

- organizational context,
- evidence,
- recommendations,
- constraints,
- organizational reasoning.

Executives should primarily confirm or adjust intent before entering Decision Lab.

The experience should feel lightweight rather than administrative.

---

# Decision Lab

Decision Lab is the canonical executive reasoning environment.

It helps executives:

- compare strategies,
- explore simulations,
- understand trade-offs,
- challenge assumptions,
- evaluate organizational impact,
- commit decisions confidently.

Decision Lab consumes canonical cognition.

It never recreates reasoning already performed by the engine.

---

# Observable Cognition

Discovery should expose genuine cognition rather than simulate artificial thought.

Executives should be able to understand:

- why Discovery reached a recommendation,
- which constraints matter,
- what assumptions exist,
- how simulations differ,
- where uncertainty remains,
- why confidence changes.

Observable cognition should increase executive trust while minimizing reading.
