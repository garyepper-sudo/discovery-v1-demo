Executive Optimization now:

* defines a transparent optimization objective,
* identifies optimization variables,
* models executive and organizational constraints,
* generates materially different bounded alternatives,
* evaluates feasibility,
* identifies binding and violated constraints,
* transparently scores every option,
* ranks feasible alternatives,
* explains why one option outranks another,
* identifies a preferred optimized option,
* preserves confidence and uncertainty,
* and exposes the tradeoffs made by optimization.

Optimization does not:

* make the final executive decision,
* execute an intervention,
* or simulate organizational outcomes.
