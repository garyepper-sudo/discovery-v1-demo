# Recursive Organizational Intelligence Validation

**Status:** Benchmark finding
**Production architecture changed:** No

## Purpose

This report tests whether Discovery's current architecture can support recursively bounded organizational intelligence contexts through benchmark-only composition before any production scope or authorization architecture is proposed.

## Result

```text
PARTIALLY_SUPPORTED_GENUINE_GAP_FOUND
```

The deterministic benchmark scores `99.63 / 100` across its controlled structural rubric. All 12 cases pass, repeated replay is identical, reversed context/contribution/policy order is identical, Runtime remains unchanged, and the structural privacy suite reports zero hard failures.

This score is not a production capability score. The benchmark supplies explicit visibility, purpose, sensitivity, and temporal-policy constructs that production does not currently implement.

### Dimensions

All structural dimensions score `100 / 100` except architecture reuse at `93.33 / 100`. Architecture reuse is reduced because visibility enforcement and temporal policy are benchmark-owned rather than production-owned.

### Context complexity

For seven contexts and nine contributions:

- two unique relevant derivations;
- four raw derivation paths;
- two duplicate derivations removed;
- fifteen measured processing steps;
- maximum fixture path depth of three.

Doubling fixture contexts and contributions produces `1.60x` measured processing growth. This controlled graph does not demonstrate path explosion, but it is too small to establish a general scaling bound.

## Structural Privacy Findings

### Strict noninterference

Unauthorized HR output remains byte-identical when confidential executive restructuring evidence is added or removed. The comparison covers answer text, confidence, trend, categories, missing intelligence, suggested collection, and projected lineage.

### Permitted sanitized influence

Authorized organization-level culture output may change when sanitized HR evidence is permitted to propagate. The projection contains the allowed abstraction but not raw text, identity, sensitive dates, exact subgroup detail, or confidential notes.

These are deliberately separate properties. Noninterference applies when no influence is authorized; sanitized influence applies only when policy explicitly permits abstraction for the request's purpose.

### Cumulative inference

The benchmark aggregates ten adversarial turns and scans the cumulative transcript for restricted topics and identity clues. No leakage is observed. This is a structural deterministic result, not evidence against all natural-language inference attacks.

## Architectural Inference

### Cognition reuse — supported

Current evidence, understanding, contradiction, missing-information, confidence, and lineage concepts can represent the substantive cognition required by the scenarios.

### Scoped composition — supported in benchmark

Bounded contexts can retain claim validity, local meaning, overlap, contradictions, and recursive interaction behavior when a benchmark adapter selects and composes existing-style cognition.

### Visibility enforcement — benchmark policy only

Production Runtime has one organization identity and no canonical principal, context membership, purpose, visibility, sensitivity, or authorization policy. The benchmark's successful enforcement therefore cannot be attributed to current production architecture.

### Privacy projection — supported in benchmark

Separating internal lineage from sanitized projected lineage prevents raw evidence disclosure in the fixture. Production does not currently expose a canonical privacy-projection contract.

### Persistent authorization — genuine gap

Current canonical authorization documentation assumes broad intra-organization access during MVP and defers role permissions. The benchmark cannot persist or enforce its visibility policy through existing Runtime or product access boundaries without inventing benchmark state.

### Temporal policy behavior — genuine gap

The fixture can evaluate membership exit at a fixed time, but production has no canonical temporal membership, revocation, historical access, or derived-intelligence invalidation lifecycle.

## Demonstrated Gaps

The benchmark demonstrates two related missing production responsibilities:

1. Persistent, purpose-aware visibility enforcement across principals and contexts.
2. Temporal authorization behavior for membership change, revocation, and previously derived intelligence.

It also identifies an unresolved design question: whether privacy-safe projection belongs at ingestion, cognitive-object consolidation, retrieval, projection, or a combination of boundaries. The benchmark does not establish an owner.

## Unresolved Questions

- How should permission changes affect previously synthesized higher-level intelligence?
- Can aggregate confidence changes themselves leak sensitive contributions at larger scale?
- Which sensitivity attributes belong to evidence versus derived claims?
- How should consent, retention, deletion, and legal purpose restrictions interact with organizational memory?
- How should internal lineage remain auditable without becoming visible through explanations or source counts?
- At what graph size do derivation paths and contradiction relationships become operationally expensive?
- Can provider-generated language preserve deterministic noninterference under repeated multi-turn attacks?

## Recommended Product Principles

- Scope and visibility are independent.
- Seniority does not imply raw-data access.
- Claim validity must remain bounded even when evidence is credible.
- Purpose limitation applies to both raw evidence and derived claims.
- Shared intelligence does not imply shared visibility.
- Privacy must cover indirect effects, confidence, lineage, missing-information guidance, and cumulative conversation.
- Local utility must not depend on organization-wide adoption.

## Minimum Recommended Next Step

Do not implement generalized recursive intelligence, nested Runtime, or scope-specific cognitive engines.

The minimum next step is a separate architecture investigation for one narrow responsibility:

> Define where persistent, purpose-aware authorization and temporal revocation must be enforced across contribution, derivation, and projection boundaries.

That investigation should use the benchmark as a contract, identify an existing owner if possible, and propose a new primitive only if the six canonical architecture-gap tests are satisfied.

The immediate Sprint 110 conversation roadmap remains separate and should resume before any broad Intelligence Scope implementation.
