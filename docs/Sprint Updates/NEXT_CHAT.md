# Next Sprint Executive Brief

**Cognitive Operating System — Adaptive Cognition and Longitudinal Strategy Validation**

Discovery has completed the Architecture Operating System and established the first generation of its Cognitive Operating System. The engine now maintains a first-class Investigation Strategy that is persisted in Runtime, projected through the Executive Projection, and driven by accumulated organizational learning rather than static heuristics.

Validate that Discovery changes **how it investigates** over multiple investigations of the same organization, not merely **what it understands**.

## Current Risks

* Investigation Strategy currently operates correctly but has only been validated using a single longitudinal learning cycle.
* Benchmark scenarios do not yet demonstrate strategy transitions such as **Explore → Preserve → Challenge → Exploit** across multiple investigations.
* Investigation Strategy is now available in the Executive Projection but has not yet been surfaced within the Executive Experience.

---

## Questions To Answer Next

1. Does Investigation Strategy reliably evolve across repeated investigations of the same organization?
2. Under what longitudinal conditions should Discovery transition between Explore, Preserve, Challenge, and Exploit modes?
3. How should Discovery explain its current cognitive strategy so executives understand not only what Discovery believes, but why it is investigating in its current way?

## Definition of Success

By the end of the next sprint Discovery should:

* Demonstrate measurable Investigation Strategy transitions across multiple investigations of the same organization.
* Expose the current Investigation Strategy and its rationale in the Executive Experience.
* Validate adaptive cognition through a dedicated Longitudinal Adaptation Benchmark while maintaining benchmark integrity and architectural governance.
