# Discovery Identity

Discovery maintains a continuously evolving **Operating Model** of an organization.

The Operating Model represents Discovery's understanding of:

- organizational reality,
- strategic objectives,
- organizational conditions,
- executive constraints,
- organizational dependencies,
- executive decisions,
- initiatives,
- historical outcomes,
- executive learning,
- persistent organizational memory.

Everything Discovery produces originates from the Operating Model.

Every recommendation, simulation, explanation, decision, and learning cycle should strengthen it.

Discovery improves executive judgment.

It does not replace executive judgment.

Discovery is **not**:

- a dashboard,
- a chatbot,
- a reporting platform,
- a document repository,
- an autonomous executive.

Discovery is an Executive Operating System powered by a continuously evolving Operating Model.

# Current Executive Experience

Discovery currently revolves around three executive experiences.

## Executive Brief

Purpose:

Help executives immediately understand:

- what Discovery believes,
- why it matters,
- what deserves attention.

Executives should spend seconds—not minutes—understanding the situation.

---

## Decision Definition

Purpose:

Confirm executive intent.

This is **not** a traditional form.

Discovery already understands:

- organizational context,
- evidence,
- constraints,
- recommendations,
- executive reasoning.

Executives primarily confirm or adjust intent before entering Decision Lab.

Future work should reduce friction until this experience feels nearly effortless.

---

## Decision Lab

Purpose:

Evaluate important executive decisions.

Decision Lab should help executives:

- compare strategies,
- challenge assumptions,
- explore simulations,
- understand trade-offs,
- evaluate organizational impact,
- commit decisions with confidence.

Decision Lab consumes existing cognition.

It should never recreate reasoning already performed by the engine.
