# Discovery Understanding Scorecard

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Baseline

**Architectural Milestone:** Cognitive Architecture Foundation

---

# Purpose

The Discovery Understanding Scorecard measures the current cognitive fitness of Discovery.

Rather than measuring software complexity, feature count, or implementation size, the scorecard evaluates Discovery's ability to transform organizational experience into enduring organizational understanding.

The scorecard establishes a baseline against which every future architectural milestone can be measured.

Its purpose is not to produce perfect scores.

Its purpose is to identify Discovery's strongest capabilities, reveal its weakest areas, and guide future architectural evolution.

---

# Scoring Philosophy

Each dimension represents a core capability of organizational cognition.

Scores represent the current maturity of Discovery rather than percentage completion.

| Score | Maturity |
|--------|----------|
| 1 | Capability does not exist |
| 2 | Experimental |
| 3 | Early implementation |
| 4 | Functional |
| 5 | Reliable |
| 6 | Strong |
| 7 | Advanced |
| 8 | Excellent |
| 9 | Best-in-class |
| 10 | Foundational capability with little room for improvement |

The objective is not to maximize every score independently.

The objective is to continuously improve Discovery's overall cognitive fitness while maintaining a coherent cognitive architecture.

# Overall Assessment

Discovery demonstrates strong capabilities in perception, explainability, reasoning, and executive communication.

Its cognitive architecture is coherent and well-defined.

Its greatest opportunities now lie in strengthening long-term organizational learning rather than improving individual investigations.

The weakest dimension is **Adaptation**.

Discovery currently preserves organizational understanding more effectively than it improves that understanding through accumulated organizational experience.

Future architectural milestones should therefore prioritize adaptive organizational understanding before introducing additional standalone capabilities.

---

# Architectural Takeaway

Discovery's progress should be measured by improvements to its Cognitive Fitness Profile rather than by feature count.

Every architectural milestone should measurably strengthen one or more dimensions of organizational cognition while preserving the coherence of Discovery's cognitive architecture.

This baseline establishes Version 1.0 of Discovery's cognitive fitness and serves as the reference point for all future architectural evolution.
