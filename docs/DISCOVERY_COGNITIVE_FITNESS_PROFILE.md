# Discovery Cognitive Fitness Profile

> **Purpose**
>
> The Discovery Cognitive Fitness Profile defines the long-term evaluation framework for Discovery.
>
> Discovery is not evaluated by the number of engines it contains or features it exposes.
>
> Discovery is evaluated by its ability to transform organizational experience into accurate, persistent, explainable, and actionable organizational understanding.
>
> Every architectural decision should improve one or more dimensions of cognitive fitness.

---

# Guiding Philosophy

Discovery is building an **Organizational Understanding Engine**, not a collection of AI features.

Every new capability should answer one question:

> **Does this improve Discovery's ability to understand organizations?**

The Cognitive Fitness Profile provides the objective framework for answering that question.

---

# The Ten Dimensions of Cognitive Fitness

## 1. Perception

### Definition

Can Discovery accurately perceive organizational signals from raw information?

Perception is the foundation of every higher cognitive capability.

Poor perception produces poor understanding regardless of later reasoning quality.

---

### Measures

- Evidence extraction
- Observation quality
- Entity recognition
- Relationship detection
- Signal detection
- Semantic normalization
- Noise filtering

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery consistently extracts the important organizational signals while ignoring irrelevant information.

---

### Long-Term Target

95–100%

---

# 2. Pattern Formation

### Definition

Can Discovery recognize recurring organizational behavior across evidence?

Organizations are driven by recurring patterns rather than isolated events.

---

### Measures

- Pattern stability
- Semantic cohorts
- Pattern recurrence
- Cross-document consistency
- Pattern coherence
- Pattern persistence

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery identifies recurring organizational dynamics rather than isolated observations.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 3. Mechanistic Reasoning

### Definition

Can Discovery explain why organizational outcomes occur?

Mechanisms describe the processes that generate observed behavior.

---

### Measures

- Mechanism inference
- Root cause quality
- Multi-step causal reasoning
- Interaction modeling
- Organizational process understanding

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery explains why organizational problems occur instead of merely describing them.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 4. Organizational Memory

### Definition

Can Discovery accumulate understanding across investigations?

Understanding should persist and improve over time.

Discovery should not repeatedly rediscover the same organizational truths.

---

### Measures

- Persistent beliefs
- Belief revision
- Historical continuity
- Memory reuse
- Knowledge preservation
- Understanding maturity

---

### Evidence of Success

Every investigation improves future organizational understanding.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 5. Concept Formation

### Definition

Can Discovery compress many observations into reusable organizational concepts?

Concepts allow Discovery to think at higher levels of abstraction.

---

### Measures

- Semantic compression
- Concept quality
- Concept stability
- Generalization
- Higher-order abstraction
- Concept hierarchy

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery produces reusable organizational concepts instead of repeatedly reasoning from raw observations.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 6. Theory Formation

### Definition

Can Discovery construct higher-order organizational theories?

A theory explains why many mechanisms exist simultaneously.

Theories become Discovery's primary organizational explanations.

---

### Measures

- Higher-order organizational theories
- Competing theories
- Theory hierarchy
- Explanatory breadth
- Predictive usefulness
- Theory stability

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery consistently explains organizations using coherent organizational theories rather than isolated mechanisms.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 7. Cognitive Integration

### Definition

Can Discovery integrate every cognitive layer into one coherent organizational understanding?

This is the bridge between many reasoning engines and one organizational brain.

---

### Measures

- Cross-layer reasoning
- Explanation chains
- Graph connectivity
- Knowledge reuse
- Consistency across reasoning layers
- Organizational synthesis

---

# 8. Executive Understanding

### Definition

Can Discovery communicate organizational understanding in executive language?

Understanding has little value if executives cannot act upon it.

---

### Measures

- Executive summaries
- Strategic recommendations
- Prioritization
- Actionability
- Communication clarity
- Decision usefulness

---

### Evidence of Success

Executives immediately understand what matters, why it matters, and what should happen next.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 9. Epistemic Intelligence

### Definition

Can Discovery reason about its own knowledge?

Discovery should understand not only what it believes, but why it believes it and what could change those beliefs.

---

### Measures

- Confidence calibration
- Competing explanations
- Missing evidence
- Falsification reasoning
- Uncertainty communication
- Belief revision quality

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery openly communicates uncertainty and identifies the evidence required to strengthen or weaken its conclusions.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

---

# 10. Emergence

### Definition

Can Discovery create understanding that never explicitly existed within the evidence?

Emergence represents the highest level of organizational cognition.

---

### Measures

- Emergent concepts
- Novel organizational theories
- Cross-domain synthesis
- Unexpected relationships
- Insight novelty
- Explanatory originality

---

### Evidence of Success

Discovery produces genuinely new organizational understanding rather than summarizing existing documents.

---

### Long-Term Target

95%

# Cognitive Maturity Model

Discovery's evolution can be viewed as stages of cognitive maturity.

## Level 1 — Observation

Discovery extracts observations from evidence.

---

## Level 2 — Pattern Recognition

Discovery recognizes recurring organizational behavior.

---

## Level 3 — Mechanistic Reasoning

Discovery explains why organizational behavior occurs.

---

## Level 4 — Organizational Reasoning

Discovery builds persistent beliefs and organizational concepts.

---

## Level 5 — Theory Formation

Discovery constructs competing organizational theories and validates them through evidence.

---

## Level 6 — Persistent Organizational Understanding

Discovery develops an evolving understanding of the organization that improves across investigations.

---

## Level 7 — Adaptive Organizational Intelligence

Discovery continuously refines its organizational understanding, identifies missing knowledge, generates executive perspectives, and improves through accumulated organizational experience.

# Architectural Principles

Maintain:

- One Organizational Memory
- One Organizational Knowledge Graph
- One Cognition Engine
- One Belief System
- One Organizational Understanding Model

Avoid:

- Duplicate reasoning engines
- Independent domain-specific cognition
- Multiple conflicting memories
- Parallel understanding systems

Discovery should think once and communicate many ways.

---

# Using the Cognitive Fitness Profile

Every sprint should begin by answering:

1. Which dimensions of cognitive fitness are weakest?
2. What architectural change will improve those dimensions?
3. How will success be measured?
4. Does the change strengthen Organizational Understanding?

Features should never be implemented solely because they are interesting.

Every capability must improve Discovery's ability to transform organizational experience into enduring organizational understanding.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Discovery's ultimate objective is not document analysis.

It is the development of an evolving organizational brain capable of:

- Learning continuously
- Explaining itself
- Revising its beliefs
- Building organizational theories
- Understanding organizations from multiple executive perspectives
- Preserving institutional knowledge
- Becoming progressively more accurate over time

The Cognitive Fitness Profile serves as the canonical framework for evaluating progress toward that vision.
